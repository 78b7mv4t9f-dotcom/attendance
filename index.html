📊 What is the 12-Month Moving Average?
The 12-month moving average is the average of the last 12 months' weekly averages, rolled forward each week. Think of it as a "smoothed trend line" — it removes the noise from individual up or down weeks (holidays, bad weather, special events) and shows you the true underlying direction of attendance over the past year. If the moving average is rising, the church is growing. If it's falling, attendance is trending down — regardless of what any single week shows. The "Moving Avg Change" column compares this year's 12-month average to last year's, giving you a clean long-term health indicator.

📊 What is the 12-Month Moving Average?
The 12-month moving average is the average of the last 12 months' weekly averages, updated each month. It smooths out short-term swings from holidays, special events, or unusual weeks, revealing the true long-term attendance trend. Rising = the church is growing. Falling = attendance is declining — independent of what any one week or month shows. The "Moving Avg Change" column compares this year's 12-month average to the same point last year, so you can see whether the long-term trend is improving or worsening year over year.

About

Data storage: All data you enter is saved in your browser's local storage on this device. Use Export/Import to keep multiple devices in sync.

Historical data: 738 records from January 2013 through April 2026 are built into the app.

Hosting: This is a single HTML file. Host it free on GitHub Pages, Dropbox, or any web server. No backend required.

Printing reports: Use the Print / Save PDF buttons to open the browser print dialog. Choose "Save as PDF" to create a file you can email to staff and elders.
